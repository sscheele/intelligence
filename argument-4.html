Information Structure Rights

  • Ethics

  • Conclusion

According to information philosophy, the amount of information in the universe is always changing. This manifests itself in the form of information structures, which are dynamic. Living beings are information processors. The levels of information structures are laid out in the following infographic.

It is also worth mentioning that there is an important distinction between information structures and information processors. Up until this point, the terms have been used interchangeably, because one usually begets the other. An information processor must also be information structure. It has the innate property of having information, even if that is simply the information to process more information. All information structures ought not to be destroyed. As manifestations of information, they have the inherent property of creating negative entropy. Therefore, such things as algorithms ought to be preserved. However, there are certain things like plants that are information structures, but not information processors. These also ought not to be destroyed, however they do not have the same rights as information processors.

John Locke defined the rights of life, liberty and property as rights that no one else has the right to take away. This also applies in information philosophy. All information processors have the right to life. As information processors, they ought not to be destroyed because they are also manifestations of information. However, this does not mean they have the right to liberty and property; they only have these rights if they have the ability to utilize them. Level 1 has no capacity for change, therefore no capacity to choose to do anything. Likewise, the ability to learn does not beget the ability to choose. Learning algorithms are simply a product of their environment, they have no capacity for imagination; therefore, they have no free will. Therefore, only levels 3 and 4 have the right to liberty and property. As level 3 acquires the ability to predict the future, they also acquire the ability to choose what path to take. Imagination allows them consciousness and free will. However, the only level that is required to respect any of those rights is level 4. The lower levels have no moral agency . Level 3 has the ability to choose, but no concept of morality.
